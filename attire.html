Chief's Attire

Agikuyu

This attire was only worn by the agikuyu leader to symbolise his/her status in the society.It demanded respect.

Maasai

Maasai leader and elder attire is deeply symbolic, representing wisdom, authority, and the preservation of culture


Warrior's Attire

Maasai

Maasai warrior (Moran) attire is defined by vibrant red, checkered, or striped garments called shuka, symbolizing bravery, blood, and unity.

Akamba

Traditional Kamba warrior (Anake) attire was designed for functionality in battle, hunting, and to signify social status and bravery,and loyalty to the tribe.

Luhya

Luhya warrior attire traditionally features animal skin garments, specifically hides worn over the shoulders or around the waist, symbolizing strength and status.


Wedding Attire

Agikuyu

Traditional Kikuyu wedding attire for ruracio (negotiation) and ngurario (final ceremony) blends cultural heritage with modern fashion,

Maasai

Traditional Maasai wedding attire is characterized by vibrant, often red, checkered cloth called shuka, worn by both men and women

Akamba

Traditional Kamba wedding attire features distinct, culturally rich outfits, often characterized by intricate beadwork, animal skins.

Giriama

Traditional Giriama wedding attire,centers on the hando (skirt) for women Men typically wear simple, matching white or colored cloth wraps

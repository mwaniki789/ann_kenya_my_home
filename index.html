Attires

Maasai Attire

This attire was and is still worn by the maasai culture. It identifies their people and is comfortable to travel long distances since the maasai are mostly pastoralists.

Agikuyu Attire

the Agikuyu attire is one that is not commonly worn out in the open especially in cities. It is mostly worn during some ceremonies such as weddings.

Pokot Attire

The pokot attire is also not worn so much in the cities. The attire is worn in the rural places where the pokot come from. It also identifies with the community.

Luhya Attire

The luhya attire is not fixed. They tend to wear different attires for different occasions. They may be the least worn attire of the many cultures.


Accessories

Maasai Accessories

Maasai accessories are vibrant, handmade beaded items (necklaces, bracelets, earrings) crafted primarily by women to signify social status, age, and cultural identity.

Giriama Accessories

Giriama accessories, originating from the coastal Mijikenda people of Kenya, are characterized by, hand-woven palm leaf items, heavy, and intricate, colorful beadwork

Kikuyu Accessories

Kikuyu traditional attire designs and photos - Tuko.co.ke Kikuyu traditional accessories prominently feature beadwork, cowrie shells, leather, and brass to symbolize status and beauty.


Practices

Circumcision ceremony

Circumcision in Kenya is a deeply rooted cultural rite of passage to manhood for most communities, practiced by roughly 84-91% of men. It signifies a transition from childhood to adulthood

Wedding Ceremony

How to Plan a Traditional Kenyan Event | 7 Success TipsWedding ceremonies in Kenya are vibrant, community-centric celebrations blending deep-rooted traditional customs with modern,often religious (Christian, Islamic, or Hindu), elements.

Naming Ceremony

Naming ceremonies in Kenya are vital, culturally rich events that introduce newborns to the community, often occurring days after birth. They typically involve honoring ancestors by naming children after grandparents

Foods

Fish

The Luo people in Western Kenya are famous for their cultural affinity for fish, specifically tilapia (ngege) and omena, which are staple foods for them

Mukimo

Mukimo is a traditional Kenyan dish originating from the Central region (Agikuyu community) that has become popular across the country

Nyama Choma

A very popular and iconic Maasai dish is Nyama Choma. It is a cornerstone of Maasai culture, particularly during ceremonies and celebrations

Matoke

A popular and staple Kisii food is Matoke (or Amatoke), which are green bananas stewed—often with garlic, onions, tomatoes, and sometimes potatoes or meat.


Economic Activities

pastoralism

Pastoralism in Kenya is a vital livelihood and economic system for over 8 million people in arid and semi-arid lands (ASALs), contributing roughly 10% to national GDP and over 50% of agricultural GDP

Fishing

Kenya offers world-class fishing, renowned for deep-sea adventures in the Indian Ocean (December–March for marlin/sailfish) and freshwater angling for Nile Perch in Lake Victoria or trout on Mt. Kenya

Economic Activities

Key Sectors | KENYA Embassy of the Republic of Kenya in JapanKenya's economy is a diverse, market-based system driven primarily by services (69% of growth), agriculture (23% of growth), and manufacturing. Key activities include tea/coffee production, horticulture, tourism, financial services, and construction.

The informal sector, which thrives in trade and small-scale services, is a major source of employment, contributing over 90% of new jobs. Key sectors include high agricultural output (coffee, tea, flowers, milk), manufacturing, tourism, and financial services.Kenya's economy in 2025 is showing strong growth, with a projected 4.8% to 5.0% expansion

Types of fishing

Trawling

Trawling: destructive fishing method is turning seafloors to ...Trawling is a widespread industrial fishing method that involves towing a cone-shaped net behind one or more boats to catch fish, shrimp, or crustaceans.

Purse Seining

Purse Seine - International Seafood Sustainability FoundationPurse seining in Kenya, commonly known as ring netting, is a highly efficient,, industrial-scale, or artisanal fishing method used to capture schooling pelagic fish like sardines, and mackerel

Hook Lining

Hook and lining is a versatile fishing method using baited hooks attached to lines, ranging from simple handheld lines to complex, long-distance commercial longlines. It targets fish by attracting them to natural or artificial bait on a hook.


Subsistence Farming

Subsistence farming is the predominant agricultural practice where over 60% of households, often on less than one acre, grow crops primarily for family consumption.

Commercial Farming

Commercial farming in Kenya is a, cornerstone of the economy, contributing 20-33% of the GDP and focusing on high-value exports like tea, coffee, flowers, and vegetables.

Mixed farming in Kenya combines crop cultivation with livestock rearing, commonly on small-scale, 2-3 acre plots to improve food security and income.
